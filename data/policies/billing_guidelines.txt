BILLING GUIDELINES — Healthcare Claim Processing Standards
==========================================================
Document ID: POL-BG-001
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Department: Revenue Cycle Management

Section 1: Purpose
-------------------
This document establishes guidelines for acceptable billing practices
in healthcare claim submissions. All providers must comply with these
standards to avoid claim denials related to billing irregularities.

Section 2: Acceptable Billing Thresholds
-----------------------------------------
A claim's billed amount must fall within a reasonable range of the
expected cost for the procedure performed. The expected cost is
determined by the standard procedure cost reference table maintained
by the payer.

  - ACCEPTABLE: Billed amount is within 3.0 times the expected cost.
  - WARNING (WARN_HIGH_BILLING): Billed amount exceeds 3.0 times
    the expected cost. Claims exceeding this threshold are flagged
    for manual review and are at high risk of denial.

Section 3: High Billing Flag — WARN_HIGH_BILLING
--------------------------------------------------
When a claim is flagged with WARN_HIGH_BILLING, the following applies:

  1. The claim is placed in a manual review queue.
  2. The provider MUST submit itemized receipts justifying the charge.
  3. Supporting documentation such as operative notes, pathology reports,
     or equipment usage logs may be required.
  4. If the provider cannot justify the billed amount within 30 days,
     the claim is automatically denied.

Remediation steps for WARN_HIGH_BILLING:
  a) Review the billed amount against the expected cost in the
     procedure cost reference table.
  b) If the higher amount is justified (e.g., complication during
     surgery, additional materials required), attach supporting
     documentation and resubmit.
  c) If the billed amount was entered in error, correct the amount
     to match the actual cost and resubmit.
  d) Contact the payer's provider relations department if the
     expected cost in the reference table is outdated.

Section 4: Overbilling Penalties
---------------------------------
Repeated WARN_HIGH_BILLING flags on claims from the same provider
may trigger a provider audit. See the Provider Audit Policy
(POL-PA-001) for audit thresholds and procedures.

Providers with a high billing flag rate exceeding 25% of total
submitted claims within a 90-day period will receive a formal
audit notification letter.
