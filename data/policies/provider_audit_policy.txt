PROVIDER AUDIT POLICY — Monitoring and Audit Triggers
=======================================================
Document ID: POL-PA-001
Effective Date: 2026-01-01
Department: Compliance and Fraud Prevention

Section 1: Purpose
-------------------
This policy defines the criteria for triggering a provider audit
based on claim submission patterns. Providers with consistently
high denial rates or billing irregularities are subject to
enhanced monitoring and formal audit proceedings.

Section 2: Provider Denial Rate Monitoring
--------------------------------------------
The provider denial rate is calculated as the percentage of a
provider's total submitted claims that have one or more validation
flags. This metric is computed in the Gold feature engineering
layer as the provider_denial_rate feature.

Thresholds:
  - NORMAL: Provider denial rate below 20%. No action required.
  - ELEVATED: Provider denial rate between 20% and 40%.
    The provider receives a notification letter recommending
    a review of their billing practices.
  - HIGH RISK: Provider denial rate above 40%.
    The provider is flagged for a formal compliance audit.

Section 3: Audit Trigger Criteria
------------------------------------
A formal provider audit is triggered when ANY of the following
conditions are met:

  1. Provider denial rate exceeds 40% over a 90-day period.
  2. More than 10 claims from the same provider are flagged with
     WARN_HIGH_BILLING within a 30-day period.
  3. More than 5 claims from the same provider are flagged with
     ERR_INCOMPLETE_CLAIM within a 30-day period.
  4. A single claim from the provider has a billed amount
     exceeding 10 times the expected cost.

Section 4: Audit Process
---------------------------
When an audit is triggered:

  a) The compliance team sends a formal audit notification to
     the provider within 5 business days.
  b) The provider has 30 days to submit documentation for all
     flagged claims.
  c) The compliance team reviews all submitted documentation
     and cross-references with clinical records.
  d) If billing irregularities are confirmed, the provider
     may face penalties including:
       - Reduced reimbursement rates for 6 months.
       - Mandatory billing training program completion.
       - Temporary suspension from the payer network.
       - Referral to the Office of Inspector General (OIG)
         for suspected fraud.

Section 5: Provider Claim Volume
-----------------------------------
The provider_claim_count feature tracks the total number of
claims submitted by each provider. High-volume providers
(more than 100 claims per month) receive additional scrutiny
because:
  - Billing errors at scale can result in significant
    financial impact.
  - High-volume providers may use automated billing systems
    that can propagate systematic errors.
  - Pattern detection is more statistically reliable with
    larger claim volumes.
